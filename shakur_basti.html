Caught Between the Railways and Poverty

Shakur Basti is a JJ cluster located in bustling West Delhi in a forgotten wedge of land between the Railways and the main road. Migrants predominantly from Uttar Pradesh and Bihar have set up their homes and lives here since the 1970s. The land however belongs to the Railways who have carried out multiple evictions even as the people returned. The most recent attempt by the railways to evict the people was particularly controversial due to the death of a toddler. The fate of the people of Shakur Basti meanwhile hangs in the balance as the case is being fought in court.

Aarzoo


F/26 . Bihar
Home maker.
26 year old mother of the toddler who died on the day of the eviction. She and her husband struggle to raise their three other children while shuttling to Tis Hazari Court for the parental negligence case filed against her husband by Delhi Police

Ramdas Ji


M/72 . UP
Daily wage labour
72 year old retired Ramdasji has dedicated his life to fighting for the rights of the poor in the city to which they contribute tremendously. He is now one of the most powerful voices of Shakur Basti and is trying to get justice for the displaced.

Harinder


M/40 . Bihar
Street Vendor
40 year old Harinder runs a Chinese fastfood stall within the Basti. His life had always featured struggles as he had his family of 5 to feed but it has taken a much harsher turn after the eviction happened.
